Congee with Mushrooms and Eggs

Congee is a super easy and filling dish made by just cooking rice longer and with more water. Instead of the normal 1:2 rice to water ratio, it is more of a 1:5. This makes it more of a rice porridge and practically anything you would eat in a regular rice bowl can be had in congee!

Ingredients

  • 2 cups Rice
  • 8-12 cups water (just be prepared to add more)
  • Mushrooms (whatever your favorites are)
  • Eggs (I usually scramble mine, but if you would like hardboiled or even these soy eggs then plan for that!)
  • Soy sauce (to taste)
  • Sesame oil (to taste)
  • Sriracha (optional)
  • Chicken stock powder (optional)

Steps

  1. First, rinse off the rice and add to pot with 5 gallons of water as well as enough chicken stock powder to your liking. Keep the rest of the water on hand, as we will add it in stages.
  2. bring to boil and then immediately lower heat to bring to a simmer
  3. Keep the simmer, adding water as it evaporates. You want to aim for a porridge consistency, looking for the rice grains to break down
  4. As that cooks, whisk up some eggs and slice the mushrooms and let sit.
  5. Right before you are ready to take the rice off the heat (about 3-5 minutes before), drop some sliced mushrooms on top of the congee and let them steam there. Scramble your eggs now as well in a small skillet to the side
  6. Once your eggs and congee are done, use a ladel to add the congee to a bowl, trying to keep the mushrooms on top. Place some egg on top and dress with soy sauce, sesame oil and sriracha to taste!
